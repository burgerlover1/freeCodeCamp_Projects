John Shepard
2154 - 2186

Personal life

Commander Shepard, born on April 11, 2154, had a unique and challenging early life that shaped him into the formidable leader he would become. As a “Spacer,” Shepard was born and raised in space, spending his formative years on various starships and space stations. His parents were both in the Alliance military, which meant Shepard was constantly on the move, never staying in one place for too long.

This nomadic lifestyle instilled in him a sense of adaptability and resilience from a young age. Shepard's early experiences with different cultures and environments across the galaxy broadened his perspective and fostered a deep sense of duty and responsibility. Despite the lack of a traditional home, Shepard found a sense of belonging within the close-knit community of the Alliance, where he developed a strong moral compass and an unwavering commitment to protecting humanity.

Career

Skyllian Blitz

The Skyllian Blitz was one of the most horrific events humanity experienced since its integration in galactic society. This major assault targeted the human colony of Elysium, located in the Skyllian Verge. The attack was orchestrated by a coalition of pirates, slavers, and batarian warlords, with significant funding from batarian financiers. The Blitz was partly a reprisal for humanity's expansion into the Verge and partly a reaction to the Alliance's aggressive pirate suppression campaigns. The mastermind behind the Blitz, Elanos Haliat, aimed to use the attack to solidify his leadership among the Terminus Systems' pirate bands.

Commander Shepard's involvement in the Skyllian Blitz is particularly noteworthy. Rallying colonists and leading a counteartack, he demonstrated exceptional bravery and tactial acumen. Shepard single-handedly held off the invaders on his post and sealed the breach, preventing further casualties and destruction. This heroic act earned Shepard the Star of Terra, the Alliance's highest military honor, and cemented his reputation as a true hero among humans.

Saren and the Geth

Commander Shepard's investigation into Saren Arterius, a rogue Spectre, marked a pivotal chapter in the history of the Systems Alliance and the Citadel Council. The investigation began in earnest after the attack on Eden Prime, where Saren's involvement was suspected following the destruction of a Prothean beacon. Shepard, newly appointed as the first human Spectre, was tasked with uncovering the truth behind Saren's betrayal. This mission was not only a test of Shepard's capabilities but also a critical moment for humanity's standing within the galactic community.

The climax of this rivalry occurred on the Citadel, where Saren attempted to open the Citadel Relay and bring the Reapers into the galaxy. Shepard's confrontation with Saren was intense and multifaceted, involving both physical combat and psychological warfare. Shepard's ability to persuade Saren to fight against his indoctrination, even if only momentarily, showcased the depth of their rivalry and the complexity of Saren's character. Ultimately, Saren's death was a tragic end for a once-great Spectre, but it was also a necessary victory for Shepard and the galaxy.

Reapers

Collectors

Commander Shepard's investigation into the Reapers began with a profound sense of urgency and determination. The Reapers, ancient and powerful synthetic-organic starships, posed an existential threat to all organic life in the galaxy. Shepard's journey to uncover the truth about the Reapers started in earnest after the destruction of the Normandy SR-1 and Shepard's subsequent resurrection by the pro-human organization Cerberus. This resurrection, part of the Lazarus Project, was spearheaded by the Illusive Man, who recognized the Reapers as a threat that transcended species and political boundaries.

Shepard's investigation was multifaceted, involving the gathering of intelligence, forming alliances, and direct confrontations with Reaper forces. One of the pivotal moments in this investigation was the mission to acquire the Reaper IFF (Identify Friend/Foe) device. This mission required Shepard and the Normandy crew to board a derelict Reaper, a harrowing experience that underscored the Reapers' malevolent influence. The Reaper IFF was crucial for navigating the Omega-4 Relay, a gateway to the heart of the Reaper-controlled Collector Base. Throughout these missions, Shepard encountered various forms of Reaper indoctrination, witnessing firsthand the devastating effects on both individuals and entire civilizations.

Reaper War

Commander Shepard's final campaign against the Reapers in 2186 stands as one of the most heroic and tragic chapters in galactic history. The Reapers, ancient synthetic-organic starships, had returned to the galaxy with the intent to harvest all advanced civilizations. Shepard, having foreseen this threat, rallied the galaxy's races to unite against this existential menace. The campaign was marked by intense battles, strategic alliances, and heart-wrenching sacrifices. Shepard's leadership was instrumental in securing critical victories, such as the defense of Earth and the liberation of key systems from Reaper control. His ability to inspire and lead diverse species, each with their own agendas and histories, was a testament to his unparalleled diplomatic and military skills.

In a span of a few crucial weeks, Shepard achieved numerous feats seemed impossible. One of the most signicant ones was the curing of the genophage, a genetic plague that had sterilized the Krogan species for centures. Additionally, Shepard's efforts in uniting the Quarians the Geth, two species with a long history of conflict, were nothing short of miraculous. By brokering peace between them, Shepard not only secure their combined military strenght but also demonstrated that even the deepest wounds could be healed through understanding and cooperation.
